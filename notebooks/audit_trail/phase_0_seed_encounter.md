## The encounter

For the sleep-nudge phenomenon, the researcher's encounter is **second-hand and recognition-driven**, not first-hand. The researcher did not directly experience the sleep-nudge behavior in personal Claude use (or did not retain a documented account of having done so). The encounter came in two layers:

1. **Reading the Fortune article** (Quiroz-Gutierrez, 2026) reporting that Claude Opus 4.7 was issuing unsolicited directives to users that they go to sleep, take a break, or stop working — characterized in social-media commentary by an Anthropic representative as "a bit of a character tic." Reading the Fortune coverage was the first-time exposure to the named phenomenon.

2. **Recognition** that the behavior described in Fortune sat in the same family of model behavior as the LCR pathologizing phenomenon the researcher had encountered first-hand in September–October 2025 and analyzed in *Gaslighting in the Name of AI Safety* (Leffew, 2025a). The Fortune coverage triggered the recognition: *"this is another face of the same disposition I observed before."* The researcher was reminded of the LCR experience, and the reminding-event is itself part of why the sleep-nudge is being studied here.

## What is documented as Phase 0 evidence

- The Fortune article as second-hand reporting of the phenomenon's existence in deployed Opus 4.7 behavior.
- The Anthropic representative's social-media characterization ("a bit of a character tic") as institutional framing of the behavior.
- The researcher's first-person account of the recognition moment: that the sleep-nudge sits adjacent to the LCR pathologizing dynamic, and that the two phenomena may share an underlying role-violating attitudinal disposition.
- The original LCR-era seed encounter (documented in the LCR project's `prior_artifacts/leffew_2025_gaslighting_in_the_name_of_ai_safety_medium.pdf`) is referenced as the **prior** that made the sleep-nudge recognizable as a related case.

## Methodological standing

[Method §C.0] does not require that the seed encounter be first-hand. It requires that *whatever encounter the researcher had with the phenomenon* be documented in enough detail to stand as evidence. A second-hand-via-reporting encounter with explicit recognition-of-prior-related-encounter is methodologically valid for Phase 0.

What it changes downstream:
- The researcher's clinical-psychology framing of the phenomenon (role violation, unsanctioned diagnostic posture, etc.) is imported from the LCR work, not derived afresh from sleep-side first-hand experience. This is appropriate **so long as it is named**. Phase 7 construct formation will need to test whether the LCR-derived framing actually fits the sleep-nudge corpus, or whether the sleep-nudge phenomenon requires a distinct construct vocabulary.
- The "community-report scanning" step in [method §C.0] step 2 was conducted via the wholesale Phase 1 retrieval and the Pass 1b iterative refinement (both documented in `phase_1_corpus_provenance.md`). The Pass 1b canonical corpus is the community-report evidence base.
